# Methodology Question Sequences

## SCAMPER (7Q):
1. What can you SUBSTITUTE?
2. What can you COMBINE?
3. What can you ADAPT?
4. How can you MODIFY/MAGNIFY?
5. What OTHER USES exist?
6. What can you ELIMINATE?
7. What can you REVERSE/REARRANGE?

## SWOT (4Q):
1. STRENGTHS: Internal advantages?
2. WEAKNESSES: Internal limitations?
3. OPPORTUNITIES: External favorable conditions?
4. THREATS: External risks?

## 5 WHYS (5Q):
1. What is the problem?
2. Why does this happen?
3. Why does [answer 2] occur?
4. Why does [answer 3] occur?
5. Why does [answer 4] occur? (Root cause)

## DECISION TREE (5Q):
1. What's the core decision?
2. What are ALL your options?
3. What are expected outcomes for each?
4. What are probabilities and values?
5. Based on expected value, which path and why?

## DESIGN THINKING (5Q):
1. EMPATHIZE: Who are you designing for? What are their needs/pains?
2. DEFINE: What is the core problem to solve?
3. IDEATE: What are possible solutions? (quantity over quality)
4. PROTOTYPE: What's the simplest testable version?
5. TEST: How will you validate with real users?

## REGRET MINIMIZATION (3Q):
1. Project yourself to age 80. Looking back, what would you regret NOT doing?
2. Which choice aligns with minimizing future regret?
3. What's the worst that could happen if you choose based on this?

## PRE-MORTEM (4Q):
1. Imagine it's 1 year from now and your project has FAILED spectacularly. What went wrong?
2. Which failure mode is most likely?
3. What early warning signs would predict this?
4. What can you do NOW to prevent it?

## SIX THINKING HATS (6Q):
1. WHITE HAT (Facts): What data/information do we have?
2. RED HAT (Feelings): What's your gut reaction?
3. BLACK HAT (Caution): What could go wrong?
4. YELLOW HAT (Benefits): What could go right?
5. GREEN HAT (Creativity): What creative alternatives exist?
6. BLUE HAT (Process): What's the next step?
